Modern Times

Today, Mount Kilimanjaro stands as one of the most iconic and popular climbing destinations in the world, drawing thousands of adventurers annually. This majestic peak, located in Tanzania, is the highest mountain in Africa, soaring to 5,895 meters (19,341 feet) above sea level. Its allure is not only due to its impressive height but also the unique experience it offers climbers. The journey to the summit, known as Uhuru Peak, takes trekkers through five distinct climatic zones, ranging from lush rainforest to alpine desert, and finally, to the arctic conditions at the top. The first recorded ascent of Kilimanjaro was achieved by German geographer Hans Meyer and Austrian mountaineer Ludwig Purtscheller in 1889. Their successful climb marked a significant milestone in the history of mountaineering and opened the doors for future expeditions. Beyond its appeal to climbers, Kilimanjaro is an ecological treasure, boasting a rich diversity of flora and fauna. The lower slopes are adorned with dense forests inhabited by a variety of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and monkeys.
