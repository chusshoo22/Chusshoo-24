Exploration

European explorers first started to document Mount Kilimanjaro in the mid-19th century. In 1848, German missionary Johannes Rebmann became the first European to report seeing the mountain, describing its snow-capped peak. His accounts, initially met with skepticism by the European scientific community, eventually piqued interest and led to further exploration. Rebmann's observations were significant as they challenged contemporary geographical and climatic understanding, sparking curiosity and subsequent expeditions. His pioneering report laid the groundwork for future explorers, such as Hans Meyer and Ludwig Purtscheller, who successfully ascended Kilimanjaro in 1889. Rebmann's contribution is thus pivotal in the mountain's documented history.
